About me



Hi I'm Erin!



That’s me with my violin, an instrument I have invested nearly 14 years, which portrays my artistic side. Still, there exist parts of me that cannot be seen through one picture. There is the academic aspect of me: I participated in a K-16 research project and published a high-impact factor journal paper with Georgia Tech students, competed in national competitions such as FBLA and pursue my scholastic goals as the AHS Science Olympiad Captain. Additionally, there is the athletic part of me that enjoys golfing as a part of the AHS Women’s Varsity Golf team. There is also the cultural side to me, who loves involving herself in various arts and cultures and exploring the world through traveling to places like Cuba and South Korea. And finally, there is me, a people person who loves all her passions not only due to the joys that come from the activities but also from the community. I have many pieces that make me who I am, and I embrace each part with love and joy.

Research Publications

Artificial Intelligent (AI) for Building Scene Data Completion

I particiapted in a K-16 project at Georiga Tech, which was formed with high-school and college undergrad students. We have published a jounal paper (Impact factor = 3.275). As a co-author, my role was to collect laser scanned data from multiple building fasades and create training data from the laser scanned data for deep-learning algortihm development. Also, I wrote the section which describes a framework of training data generation process with raw and sythetic data. I was also in charge for proofreading the manuscript.

Chen, J., Yi, S., Kahoush, M., Cho, E. , and Cho, Y.(2020). "Point Cloud Scene Completion of Obstructed Building Facades with Generative Adversarial Inpainting." Sensors, 20(18), 5029 doi.org/10.3390/s20185029


Artificial Intelligent (AI) for Building Scene Data Completion

I particiapted in a Georiga Dept. of Transportation (GDOT) project at Georiga Tech. The project aims to develop an Internet of Things (IoT) safety sensing device which alerts construction equipment operators and ground workers in proximity to improve safety in roadway workzones. The report is published at a Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) database. As a co-author of the report, my role was to analyze the collected sensor data to evaluaate the performace of the developed sensor systems by comparing the ground truth from video recordings with the sensor's alert data records. Also, I proofread the report.

Cho,Y., Kim, K.N., Jeong, I., Chen, J., Kim, P., and Cho, E. (2020). "Phase III: Smart Proximity Work Zone Safety Technology Deployment." Final report(#FHWA-GA-20-1817, 18-17), Georgia DOT
